About

PhD candidate in synthetic organic chemistry with integrated expertise in computational chemistry and data science. Dissertation research in the Romero group at Baylor University focused on developing Zr–H catalyzed reductive two-fragment coupling reactions to form C–N, and C–C bonds. Expertise in method development, high-throughput experimentation, multi-step synthesis, characterization, high-throughput computational workflows, data science and machine learning. Collaborative track record across synthesis and computation teams with published works.
